Content in Question

SIS is a student information system application sourced by the majority of accredited universities in the United States.

As a central source of information for students across the country, the experience should be easy, efficient, and valuable.

Currently, the design of SIS is cluttered, which deters students from wanting to use the site, let alone from findingthe information they need.

Target Users

SIS is used in all departments of the Johns Hopkins University, from the graduate schools to the research institusions to the undergratuate population.

The purpose is to provide a centralized space for all university related information.

Step 1: Contextual Inquiry

Task 1

~Users 1 and 2 were asked to explore the Menu items on the top left of the page

The users first both checked their course schedules and grades. They both experienced no trouble performing these tasks; these are the actions performed most frequently on SIS.

The users were then asked to navigate to and create Degree Audits. User 1 had trouble navigating to the correct page, and User 2 expressed that she experienced trouble with this in the past, sometimes it would fail to create, and when it did create the information was incorrect.

Task 2

~User 2 was asked to check her student loan status.

User 2 was able to navigate to the page, but shared that the information is only shown in "history of payment" format, mixing together debit and credit payments. She keeps a running total elsewhere, otherwise she would have to keep adding the numbers manually.

Task 3

~User 1 was asked to perform a class search.

She was able to do this, but not without error.

She frequently runs into the issue of "term" being unselected and wishes it would default to the current semester.

In addition, when searching for a course that does not fulfull a major requirement, it can be difficult to sift through all of the courses being offered to find one of interest.

On top of this, sometimes, when a course is searched for via number rather than name it fails to show as available.
